Projects

ALASA  (https://alasa.org.za)

The African Language Association of Southern Africa (ALASA) was established in 1979 as a non-discriminatory, non-profit-making association, to promote interaction between researchers, students and teachers of African languages and literature, and to assist in promoting research, language development and effective verbal and written communication in a multilingual society. The journal is the official mouthpiece of the African Language Association of Southern Africa (ALASA), established in 1979. 

AAFLL (https://aafll.co.za) 

African Association for Forensic and Legal Linguists (AAFLL) The AAFLL is an international organization devoted to the little-known field of forensic linguistics. In South Africa and Africa in general, law and language are research and speciality areas.
The AAFL’s areas of specialization include language use in police investigation interviews, the legal system, the language used in courtrooms and as evidence, and the analysis and translation of legal writings.

CPUT Online Mutlingual Glossaries (http://mlg.cput.ac.za)  

The CPUT Online Multilingual Glossaries, is an  on-going project which focuses on subject-specific terminology with the aim of promoting multilingual teaching and learning of key concepts. This initiative  also contribute to the development of scientific terminologies in African languages for different study fields. Acknowledgements: with glossary authors, the language coordinators, lectures, students, language specialists and industry personnel.

Zanele Muholi is a visual activist, humanitarian and art practitioner who focuses on the documentation and celebration of the lives of South Africa’s Black lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer and intersex communities.
